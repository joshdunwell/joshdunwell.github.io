Will lack of experience hurt promotion hopes?

Investigating Latics squad age problem further

Shaun Maloney's Wigan Athletic face a challenging season ahead in League One with a squad heavily reliant on youth and lacking in seasoned, older players. While young talent can bring energy, dynamism, and fresh ideas to the pitch, the lack of experienced heads could pose significant problems, especially in the grind of a long League One campaign. In a league known for its physicality, tactical astuteness, and high-pressure situations, having a core of veteran players is often crucial to steadying the ship during difficult periods. Experienced players provide leadership, resilience, and a sense of calm in critical moments, which can be pivotal in tight matches or when navigating a promotion push. Without a strong presence of such players, Wigan may struggle with consistency, decision-making, and the ability to manage games against more streetwise, battle-hardened opponents. The challenges of League One require a delicate balance between youthful exuberance and seasoned professionalism, and Maloney’s young squad might find it hard to maintain form over the course of the season. While there is undoubtedly potential within the ranks, the lack of experienced campaigners could ultimately hinder Wigan’s ambitions of returning to the Championship at the first attempt.

Wigan Athletic could greatly benefit from signing older, experienced free-agent strikers to bolster their attacking options and provide invaluable mentorship to their younger forwards. An experienced striker brings a wealth of knowledge, game intelligence, and a proven track record in high-pressure situations, which are critical for a promotion push in League One. Their presence would not only strengthen the squad’s depth but also serve as a learning opportunity for the younger players, helping them develop their positional awareness, finishing techniques, and tactical understanding. Such a signing could bridge the gap between potential and performance, providing both immediate impact and long-term growth for Wigan's strike force.
